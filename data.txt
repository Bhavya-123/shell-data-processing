			<h1 class='post-title'>5 Great Short Story Websites You Can Read for Free</h1>
		</div>
		<div class="post-content">
			
<p>You might wonder why anyone would bother building or talking about short story websites these days. </p>



<p>I mean, here in 2019, there is no shortage of options when it comes to satisfying your hunger as a reader.</p>



<p>Most everybody has access to an eReader of one sort or another, whether it&#8217;s a physical Kindle or Nook, or the app versions of those readers that run on your phone or tablet (or even your PC).</p>



<p>And if you&#8217;re part of the eReader resistance, you can still buy physical books at outfits like Barnes &amp; Nobles and Half-Price Books, or you can order them from Amazon or other online retailers.</p>



<p>Heck, if you&#8217;re <strong>really </strong>adventurous or cantankerous, you can even visit your local library. Imagine!</p>



<p>But as great as all those sources are, they&#8217;re not the whole ball of wax, especially if you&#8217;re a fan of short stories, like I am.</p>



<p>No matter how many epic novels you gorge yourself on or how many series of smaller novels you binge read, there are certain things they won&#8217;t give you, but that short stories will.</p>



<p>For instance, short stories let you &#8230;</p>



<ul><li>Read a complete tale in just a few minutes</li><li>Sample lots of authors in a short period of time</li><li>Visit lots of <em>worlds </em>in a short period of time</li><li>Move on if you don&#8217;t like a story, without losing a huge investment of time</li><li>Stoke your imagination and leave you wondering &#8230; what next?</li><li>Delight you with unexpected twists</li><li>Leave you breathless with rapid-fire action and epic, flourishing endings</li></ul>



<p>And a lot more, too.</p>



<p>But there are so many short stories out there, it&#8217;s sometimes hard to pick out the ones you would really want to read from the  ones that you would consider just noise.</p>



<p>And that&#8217;s where short story <em>websites </em>come in.</p>



<p>They curate a certain type or quality of short story and serve them up to you on the regular. The ones I talk about below do it all for free, too.</p>



<p>The hope is that you&#8217;ll find an author or two you like and become lifetime devotees of those writers and the websites who feature them. </p>



<p>That&#8217;s certainly been the case for me with these great short story websites &#8212; they&#8217;ve become part of my home on the web, and I hope you&#8217;ll love them as much as I do. So, let&#8217;s dig in, sit a spell, and read some wonderful short stories.</p>



<p>(And check back here, as I&#8217;ll be adding to the list over time.)</p>



<h2><a href="https://www.ropeandwire.com/">Rope and Wire</a></h2>



<p><a href="https://www.ropeandwire.com/"><em>Rope and Wire</em></a><em> </em>is my go-to site when I&#8217;m in the mood for a western, which is pretty often.</p>



<p>Here you&#8217;ll find an array of short stories from around 50 different western authors and covering just about every angle of the genre you could imagine. </p>



<p><em>Rope and Wire</em> puts up new stories on a regular basis and keeps a running &#8220;scoreboard&#8221; of the most popular tales each month. </p>



<p>If you long for the open range, you really need to check out  <a href="https://www.ropeandwire.com/">this site</a>. </p>



<p>(<strong>Full disclosure:</strong> one of my short stories is published on <em>Rope and Wire</em>.)</p>



<h2><a href="http://flashfictiononline.com/main/">Flash Fiction Online</a></h2>



<p>If you&#8217;re into tight prose that packs a mighty punch in a small package, you&#8217;re probably already a fan of flash fiction. But just in case you&#8217;re not familiar with this form of short story, a flash fiction narrative typically checks in at fewer than 1000 words but still tells a complete tale.</p>



<p>As a writer, it&#8217;s a challenging way to tell a story.</p>



<p>As a reader, it&#8217;s a delightful way to tweak your literary mind in almost no time at all.</p>



<p><em>Flash Fiction Online </em>produces a monthly magazine, which you can buy in electronic form. It&#8217;s worth the 99 cents, but &#8230;</p>



<p><em>FFO </em>also makes many of their stories available on their website for free. These tales span a wide variety of genres, and you&#8217;re sure to find several that fit your tastes.</p>



<p>Visit <em>Flash Fiction Online</em> <a href="http://flashfictiononline.com/main/">here</a>.</p>



<h2><a href="https://www.creepypasta.com/">CreepyPasta</a></h2>



<p>Creepypasta is one of my guilty reading pleasures in general, and <a href="https://www.creepypasta.com/">CreepyPasta,com</a> is the place I most often go to satisfy my dark cravings.</p>



<p>For the uninitiated, creepypasta is basically a genre of horror stories that originate and proliferate on the internet. The name comes from &#8220;copypasta,&#8221; a term that denotes the common web practice of copy-and-pasting text or pictures from one source to another.</p>



<p>It&#8217;s basically the way stuff becomes &#8220;viral.&#8221;</p>



<p>Add some dark short stories to the formula and you have &#8220;creepypasta.&#8221;</p>



<p>At CreepyPasta.com, you&#8217;ll find a frenzied variety of scary stories, posted at the rate of one or more per day. The site allows readers to rate each story, so you&#8217;ll have an idea about the quality of a piece by its overall score.</p>



<p>Be aware that some of the stories on CreepyPasta.com are a little rough, not necessarily family-friendly or safe for work. If you can put your squeamishness aside in the name of some spine-tingling tales, though, you can visit CreepyPasta.com <a href="https://www.creepypasta.com/">here</a>.</p>



<h2><a href="https://dailysciencefiction.com/">Daily Science Fiction</a></h2>



<p>If you like science fiction, and especially <em>short </em>science fiction, then you pretty much <strong>have </strong>to read <em>Daily Science Fiction</em> on a regular basis.</p>



<p>Like <em>Flash Fiction Online</em>, this website focuses on really short stories &#8212; 100 to 1500 words, according to their submission guidelines. However, they also publish &#8220;flash series&#8221; of short stories that each stand on their own but that connect to each other in some way.</p>



<p>If you don&#8217;t want to <strong>visit </strong>the <em>Daily Science Fiction</em> site every day, you can also sign up to have the new stories delivered to your email address.</p>



<p>Either way, this is a great place to find prolific sci-fi output in the tradition of Asimov. </p>



<p>Check out <em>Daily Science Fiction</em> <a href="https://dailysciencefiction.com/">here</a>.</p>



<h2><a href="https://americanliterature.com/">AmericanLiterature.com</a></h2>



<p>AmericanLiterature.com has been around for almost 13 years now, an eternity for a website.</p>



<p>There&#8217;s a good reason for its longevity, too &#8212; it presents some of the greatest stories ever penned by American authors (and some with Old World origins).</p>



<p>From children&#8217;s tales like <em>The Velveteen Rabbit</em> to poems by Robert Frost and Robert Burns to a collection of short stories from O. Henry, just about every literary taste has a home at <a href="http://AmericanLiterature.com">AmericanLiterature.com</a>.</p>



<p>The only downsides I can see are that the site is not as focused as some of the more genre-specific entries on this list and that the stories have been around for ages.</p>



<p>But if you want classic stories, websites don&#8217;t come much better than <a href="http://AmericanLiterature.com">this one</a>.</p>
